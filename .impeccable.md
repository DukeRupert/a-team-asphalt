## Design Context

### Users
Homeowners and commercial property managers in Western Washington's Pierce and King counties (Sumner, Puyallup, Auburn, Bonney Lake, Tacoma metro) seeking asphalt paving, sealcoating, striping, and repair. They arrive looking for a local, credible contractor — proof of experience, clear services, and a fast path to a free estimate. Many have been burned by fly-by-night operators and arrive skeptical. Both residential and commercial audiences carry equal weight.

### Brand Personality
**Bold, Earned, Straight.** This is a 30+ year family-owned asphalt company co-owned by Gary Jorgensen and Don Lee out of Sumner, WA. The voice is a craftsman who knows his trade cold and has nothing to prove — direct, confident without being cocky, always on the customer's side. The A-Team name is a gift: lean into the reference occasionally but never make it a gimmick. Tagline: "As good as the best, different than the rest."

**Voice rules:** Short sentences. Plain words. Say the true thing. No "solutions," "leverage," "seamless," "journey," "passionate about asphalt," or any corporate filler. We're a crew, not a "team of professionals." See `docs/brand-guide.md` for the full banned word list, tone dials, and voice checklist.

**Emotional goals:** Visitors should feel confidence and trust (this crew has done this for decades), straightforward reliability (no-nonsense, what-you-see-is-what-you-get), and modern competence (a tight operation that's current, not stuck in the past).

### Aesthetic Direction

**NOTE: The placard/champagne-gold concept (`/placard` routes, Zilla Slab, warm metallics) is PAUSED. The active theme is Industrial (dark) / Cool Steel (light). All design work targets these two stylesheets.**

- **Visual tone**: Clean industrial aesthetic — high contrast, bold typography, cool chrome silvers and grays. The design is modern and direct, like clean machinery. Zone-based sections with labeled entry points (eyebrow labels) and swooping red dividers between content areas.
- **Theme system**: Two CSS files swapped via `localStorage` preference or `prefers-color-scheme`:
  - **Dark mode** (`static/css/industrial.css`): Near-black backgrounds (`#1A1A1A`), silver/chrome text, subtle SVG noise texture
  - **Light mode** (`static/css/cool-steel.css`): Cool steel grays (`#F4F5F6` base), dark text, same structural language. Hero and footer sections remain dark to anchor the brand.
- **Color palette**:
  - **Dark mode**: `--black: #111111`, `--asphalt: #1A1A1A` (bg), `--charcoal: #2E2E2E`, `--silver: #C0C0C0` (secondary text), `--chrome: #E8E8E8`, `--white: #F5F5F5` (primary text)
  - **Light mode**: `--steel-50: #F4F5F6` (bg), `--steel-200: #D5D9DD` (borders), `--steel-400: #7E8A96` (muted), `--steel-600: #3D4850` (body), `--steel-800: #1C2428` (headings), `--steel-900: #0E1517`
  - **Shared accent**: `--red: #C8102E` (CTAs, accent rules, hover), `--red-dk: #9E0B22`, `--red-hover: #E01233`
  - **Dark context vars** (used in light mode for hero/footer): `--dark-bg: #1C2428`, `--dark-text: #B0B7BF`, `--dark-headline: #F5F5F5`
  - **Critical distinction from paused concept**: The metallic accent is cool chrome silver, NOT warm champagne gold. Chrome reads modern/industrial. This coolness is intentional and must be consistent.
- **Typography** (all sizes use `:root` tokens):
  - Display (hero, section titles): **Bebas Neue** — `--type-hero` / `--type-display`, tracking `--track-display`.
  - Navigation, labels, buttons: **Oswald** 500-700, UPPERCASE. `--type-nav` (13px), `--type-label` (11px), `--type-ui` (14px). Tracking: `--track-nav` (0.15em), `--track-eyebrow` (0.35em), `--track-label` (0.2em).
  - Body / functional: **Source Sans 3** 300-600. `--type-body` (16px), `--type-small` (12px).
- **Timing tokens**: `--duration-fast` (0.15s) for micro-interactions, `--duration-med` (0.25s) for menu/reveal.
- **Red overlays**: Use `rgba(var(--red-rgb), opacity)` — never hard-code `rgba(200, 16, 46, ...)`.
- **Signature elements**:
  - **Clipped-corner buttons**: `clip-path: polygon(...)` on primary and secondary buttons — no border-radius anywhere. This is the most distinctive UI detail.
  - **Swooping dividers**: 3px bar with red gradient on left third and right-pointing arrow triangle. Marks transitions between content zones.
  - **Section eyebrows**: Oswald 11px UPPERCASE, wide tracking, red text, with animated horizontal line rule extending right.
  - **Asymmetric service grid**: Featured card large (60% left, spans 3 rows), 3 stacked cards right (40%). Cards have hover accent triangle top-right.
  - **SVG noise texture**: Subtle feTurbulence overlay on dark sections for tactile depth.
  - **Credential badges**: License, insurance, BBB rating prominently displayed in hero credentials bar.
- **Patterns to maintain**: Square corners everywhere (no border-radius). Labeled zone sections. Red reserved for action/accent only. Dark hero/footer even in light mode. Subtle noise textures.
- **Anti-references**: Generic contractor templates, stock photo-heavy layouts, rounded corners and soft gradients, Wix/Squarespace default themes, warm champagne/gold palettes (that's the paused placard concept), condensed gothic/military stencil type.

### Design Principles
1. **Red means action** — `#C8102E` is reserved for CTAs, accent rules, hover states, and the hero "A" letter. Never diluted across large surface areas.
2. **Labeled zones, not free-flowing content** — Every section has an eyebrow label entry, defined content area, and swooping divider exit. Clear structural rhythm throughout.
3. **Typography is the identity** — No logomark. Bebas Neue for impact, Oswald for structure, Source Sans 3 for clarity. The type treatment IS the brand.
4. **Clipped corners are the signature** — The `clip-path` on buttons and select elements is the most distinctive UI detail. Never replace with border-radius.
5. **Dark anchors the brand** — Hero and footer remain dark in both themes. These bookend sections carry the industrial identity regardless of mode.
6. **Chrome, not gold** — Cool silver/chrome is the accent metallic. This reads modern and industrial. Warm tones belong to the paused placard concept.
7. **Honest and direct** — Copy and UI should sound like a craftsman talking. Short sentences. Plain words. The customer's interest is visibly prioritized.
8. **Mobile doesn't mean lesser** — Responsive breakpoints maintain bold typographic impact and structural clarity, not collapse into generic stacked layouts.
9. **Accessibility: WCAG AA** — Standard contrast ratios, keyboard navigation, screen reader support. Both dark and light palettes must meet AA contrast minimums. `prefers-reduced-motion` respected.
