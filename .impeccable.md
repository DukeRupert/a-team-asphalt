## Design Context

### Users
Homeowners and commercial property managers in Eastern Washington seeking asphalt paving, sealcoating, striping, and repair services. They arrive looking for a local, credible contractor — they want to see proof of experience, understand services offered, and request a free estimate quickly. Both residential and commercial audiences carry equal weight.

### Brand Personality
**Bold, Confident, Gritty.** This is a 30-year-old family-owned asphalt company that has outlasted competitors by doing the work right. The voice is direct and unapologetic — no corporate polish, no marketing fluff. Think construction site confidence: "We show up, we do it right, we don't cut corners." The A-Team name carries action-hero swagger ("I love it when a plan comes together") that should be leaned into, not away from.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Industrial, high-contrast, typographically bold. Heavy use of condensed uppercase type (Barlow Condensed 800-900), tight leading, structured grids with hard borders.
- **Color palette**: Red (#CC1A1A / #C41B1B) as the primary accent, black/near-black as the anchor, silver/chrome tones for secondary text. Minimal color — let contrast and typography do the work.
- **Typography**: Barlow Condensed for headlines (weight 700-900, uppercase, tight tracking), Barlow for body (400-700). Outlined/stroked type used sparingly for visual texture.
- **Patterns**: Grid lines, diagonal stripes, corner triangle accents, scanline overlays, numbered service cards with ghost numerals. These give a blueprint/technical drawing quality.
- **Current state**: Three concepts are being refined for client presentation — Dark Nostalgia (cinematic dark), Clean Industrial (structured light, editorial), and Editorial Bold (warmer, approachable light). All three should be treated as active design directions. Design improvements should be applicable across concepts where possible.
- **Anti-references**: Generic contractor templates, stock photo-heavy layouts, rounded corners and soft gradients, anything that looks like a Wix/Squarespace default theme.

### Design Principles
1. **Typography is the hero** — With no photography yet, bold condensed type, scale contrast, and structural layout carry all visual weight. Every heading should feel like it could be stamped on steel.
2. **Structured, not decorated** — Use borders, grids, and spatial rhythm instead of ornament. Whitespace is earned through density elsewhere.
3. **Red means action** — The red accent is reserved for CTAs, hover states, and key emphasis. Never diluted across large surface areas (except the marquee band, which is intentional).
4. **Honest and direct** — Copy and UI should be blunt and confident. No "solutions," no "leverage" — just "paving," "estimates," and "let's get to work."
5. **Mobile doesn't mean lesser** — Responsive breakpoints should maintain the bold typographic impact, not collapse into generic stacked layouts.
