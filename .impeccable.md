## Design Context

### Users
Homeowners and commercial property managers in Western Washington's Pierce and King counties (Sumner, Puyallup, Auburn, Bonney Lake, Tacoma metro) seeking asphalt paving, sealcoating, striping, and repair. They arrive looking for a local, credible contractor — proof of experience, clear services, and a fast path to a free estimate. Many have been burned by fly-by-night operators and arrive skeptical. Both residential and commercial audiences carry equal weight.

### Brand Personality
**Bold, Earned, Straight.** This is a 20+ year family-owned asphalt company run by Gary A. Jorgensen Jr. out of Sumner, WA. The voice is a craftsman who knows his trade cold and has nothing to prove — direct, confident without being cocky, always on the customer's side. The A-Team name is a gift: lean into the reference occasionally ("I love it when a plan comes together" as an easter egg) but never make it a gimmick. The show's applicable DNA: competent, resourceful, loyal to the client, a little irreverent. Tagline: "As good as the best, different than the rest."

**Voice rules:** Short sentences. Plain words. Say the true thing. No "solutions," "leverage," "seamless," "journey," "passionate about asphalt," or any corporate filler. We're a crew, not a "team of professionals." See `docs/brand-guide.md` for the full banned word list, tone dials, and voice checklist.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Placard / credential plate aesthetic derived from Gary's actual business card. Zone-based sections with labeled entry points (eyebrow labels), double-rule borders, and warm metallics. The card's structure is a placard — formal, authoritative, earned — not a flyer or a brochure. Think military service record meets western poster typography.
- **Color palette (from the card)**:
  - Near-black base: `#141414` (page bg — not pure black)
  - Dark band: `#1E1C18` (nav, section headers, badge backgrounds)
  - **Champagne gold**: `#C8B882` (light: `#DDD0A0`, dark: `#9A8C60`) — the card's soul. Reads as *earned*, not flashy. Used for display type, borders, decorative rules, owner name treatments.
  - **A-Team Red**: `#C0200A` (bright: `#D93010`, muted: `#8B1508`) — company name, CTAs, accent rules, hover states.
  - Off-white / warm cream: `#F2EFE6` — body text on dark, contact details.
  - Warm mid-gray: `#6A6560` — muted labels, secondary text, dividers.
  - **Critical distinction**: The secondary metallic is warm champagne gold, NOT cool chrome silver. Cool chrome reads modern/industrial. Warm gold reads established and earned. This warmth must come through in every element.
- **Typography**:
  - Display (company name, hero, section titles): **Zilla Slab** 700 — slab serif matching the card's western poster character. High x-height, strong stroke contrast.
  - Secondary display (owner name, subheadings): **Oswald** 500-700, uppercase, tracked wide.
  - Body / functional: **Barlow** 400-600.
  - Condensed accents: **Barlow Condensed** 600-700 for specific treatments.
  - Key type treatments from the card: the "owner" eyebrow badge (small all-caps label in dark band), double-rule borders, dotted separators, rotated vertical wordmark.
- **Patterns & texture**: Subtle diagonal stripe texture at very low opacity on dark sections. Double-rule border system (the card's most distinctive structural element). Dotted separators between content zones. Square corners on buttons — no border-radius.
- **Light mode**: Equally important as dark mode. Both modes should fully express the placard/champagne-gold aesthetic. Light mode inverts the value scale but maintains the same warmth, gold accents, and structural language.
- **Current state**: The UI guide (`docs/ATEAM-UI-GUIDE.MD`) is THE design direction. All existing concepts (Dark Nostalgia, Clean Industrial, Editorial Bold) should conform to this placard/credential plate aesthetic. The guide includes complete component specs for nav, hero, trust band, service cards, buttons, forms, and more.
- **Anti-references**: Generic contractor templates, stock photo-heavy layouts, rounded corners and soft gradients, anything that looks like a Wix/Squarespace default theme, cool chrome/silver palettes, condensed gothic/military stencil type.

### Design Principles
1. **The card is canon** — Gary's business card is the source of truth for the visual identity. The placard structure, champagne gold warmth, slab serif type, and zone-based layout all derive from it. When in doubt, reference `docs/ATEAM-UI-GUIDE.MD`.
2. **Labeled zones, not free-flowing content** — Every section has a clear container with an eyebrow label entry, defined content, and ruled exit. No content bleeds ambiguously between sections. This is the card's placard logic translated to web.
3. **Red means action** — A-Team Red (`#C0200A`) is reserved for the company name, CTAs, hover states, and accent rules. Never diluted across large surface areas without intention.
4. **Warmth is earned** — The champagne gold reads as time-in-trade, not decoration. Use it for borders, display type, and credential treatments. It's the brand's soul — don't substitute it with cool silver or generic gray.
5. **Typography is the identity** — There is no logomark. The type treatment IS the identity. Zilla Slab for authority, Oswald for structure, Barlow for clarity. Every heading should feel like it could be stamped on a credential plate.
6. **Honest and direct** — Copy and UI should sound like a craftsman talking, not a brand consultant's version of one. Short sentences. Plain words. The customer's interest is visibly prioritized over ours.
7. **Mobile doesn't mean lesser** — Responsive breakpoints should maintain the bold typographic impact and placard structure, not collapse into generic stacked layouts.
8. **Accessibility: WCAG AA** — Standard contrast ratios, keyboard navigation, screen reader support. The champagne-on-dark palette must meet AA contrast minimums.
